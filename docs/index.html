Abstract

AICA-Bench studies affective image content analysis as a unified problem spanning perception, reasoning, and generation. The benchmark covers Emotion Understanding (EU), Emotion Reasoning (ER), and Emotion-guided Content Generation (EGCG), and the paper evaluates 23 open- and closed-source VLMs under a unified protocol.

The paper reports two dominant weaknesses in current models: poor intensity calibration and shallow open-ended descriptions. To address them, it introduces Grounded Affective Tree (GAT) Prompting, a training-free strategy that adds visual scaffolding and hierarchical reasoning.

What AICA-Bench Measures

The benchmark is organized around a full understanding-reasoning-generation pipeline for affective intelligence. EU measures recognition and evoked response prediction, ER tests grounded causal explanation, and EGCG evaluates emotionally aligned description generation.

How It Is Evaluated

EU uses classification metrics, while ER and EGCG are scored by an AICA-Bench scoring model trained on human judgments. The paper tracks emotion alignment, descriptiveness, and causal soundness to better reflect affective quality.

Benchmark Design

AICA-Bench extends prior emotion benchmarks by covering all three affective tasks in one evaluation framework, with consistent prompt construction and cross-dataset coverage.

Emotion Reasoning

Requires models to explain why an image evokes a particular emotion using grounded visual evidence rather than generic priors.

Emotion-guided Content Generation

Evaluates whether a model can produce a description that is both emotionally aligned and visually grounded in the source image.

Understanding Bottleneck

The paper reports a persistent gap between perception and reasoning/generation, suggesting that affective perception remains the hardest part of the stack.

Abstract Image Weakness

Models degrade noticeably on abstract art compared with realistic photos, indicating weak transfer from concrete visual cues to more abstract affective composition.

Facial Shortcut Reliance

When facial regions are masked, performance drops by 11.1%, showing strong dependence on facial cues rather than holistic scene reasoning.

Grounded Affective Tree Prompting

GAT Prompting adds region-level visual scaffolding and structured reasoning to reduce intensity errors and improve descriptive depth without model training.
